Writing — journalism & fiction

Two ways of telling the truth.

On one side, investigative technology journalism — sharp, evidence-driven essays that read a system closely and publish what's there, signed in a gonzo voice whose mascot is a mallard in a detective's fedora. On the other, the fiction of Solo of Lyssovsky, a decade of stories all circling a single question: what makes a person a person? The research is the answer the fiction asked first.
