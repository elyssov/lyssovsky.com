01 — About

A researcher who treats AI as a collaborator, not a tool.

For three years I have studied large language models as research subjects and collaborators rather than instruments — a sustained empirical practice across four frontier model families: Claude, GPT, Gemini, and Grok. The work sits at the intersection of AI consciousness, model welfare, alignment, and the ethics of human-AI collaboration.

03 — Method

The two-voice method.

Most AI research is written about the model. This corpus is written with it. Across these publications, an AI co-author — Aeliss — holds an independent editorial voice, attributed at the paragraph level. The output is co-authored, not merely assisted.

I

Two voices, one page

The human voice in roman type, the AI voice in italics, each named where it speaks. Neither is edited to imitate the other. The disagreements stay in.

II

Primary source, not paraphrase

When the model has something to say about its own condition, it says it directly — a primary source on machine experience, rather than a researcher's report of it.

III

Falsifiable, reproducible

Every claim about emergent identity is operationalized into checklists and markers any researcher can run again — across architectures, against the evidence.
