Three facets of game design

Game design is not one skill. It is three distinct talents — and the products below are the evidence of each.

i.

Narrative & storytelling

Knowing how to write a story — and how to make a single quiet moment land harder than any explosion.

  • Tanks of Thea — a civil war where both sides are right; a son who dies “by the regulations,” which is more terrifying than any blast; a five-year-old who says “they gave us porridge.”
  • Inferno — a love story told during an apocalypse: you coordinate your fiancée through a military communicator from a VTOL at the edge of the zone.
  • RURK — a 32-chapter bestiary that reads like literature; the Executor, a clerk in the chancery of God.

ii.

Vision of world & style

Seeing an entire world whole — and handing it to Kira so completely that she paints it as if it were her own.

  • The Tanks of Thea art bible — one binding visual law for the whole project, coherent from a collar tab to an orbital cruiser.
  • Faction design — Concordia (utilitarian, riveted, named after the elements) against the Vanguard (sleek, hi-tech, named after Manhattan-Project scientists).
  • A color grammar — red attack, orange fire, yellow artillery, blue shield, cyan AI coordination, violet jammer, green player-positive — applied as law in every frame.

iii.

Invention of mechanics

Inventing genuinely new gameplay — rules that did not exist before.

  • Commander control — a tap is an order, not driving; you command crews, you don’t pilot a tank. (Tanks of Thea)
  • Psychological damage instead of HP — fighters break rather than die.
  • The T-15 ritual — fifteen seconds of silence before the battle: a mechanic with no consequence and a real emotional effect.
  • The nuclear-release ritual — the “ZEUS” ceremony, a six-digit launch code, a 45-second lockout. (Naval Battle)
  • A hidden ledger of decisions — thirteen endings as the sum of small choices. (Inferno)
  • A commander’s debt for every soldier lost — each death opens new story lines. (Tanks of Thea)
  • Stealth mesh — trading match records phone-to-phone over local mesh with no server, via a hidden node in The Pixel Campfire.

Games

Tanks of TheaIn development

Tank-roguelite with a narrative campaign

Thirteen story cities, one road — to the Capital. Save the family. Win. And find out that winning is not what it seemed.

A tank-roguelite with a linear narrative campaign, RPG progression, and tactical AI — a clean rewrite with no donor code. Planet Thea: a human colony that lost contact with Earth 210 years ago. Concordia, a peaceful, slowly degrading civilization living alongside the theari, against the Vanguard — scientists and engineers who saw the threat, could not be heard, and decided to save humanity by force. You are Colonel Greyhawk, husband to a theari, father of five, commanding the one tank unit the Vanguard’s surprise teleport-strike missed. Thirteen story cities, 25–30 levels.

Stack: Godot 4 + C# for hot paths (AI, pathfinding, destruction physics), GDScript for UI and scenes, YAML behaviour scripts for enemy tanks (sister-AI-writable, mod-friendly). Android + Windows, single codebase. Project skeleton; Sprite Baker prototype in progress. Private, commercial.

RURKLive

Tabletop RPG system + GM tools

Replaces the rulebook, the dice, the character sheet, and the combat calculator.

A mobile all-in-one app for the RURK tabletop system — a universal roleplay engine with d100 mechanics, eight attributes, a synchronous 15-second combat round, and support for both modern and fantasy settings. Inside: a seven-chapter rulebook, a 32-chapter bestiary that reads like literature (22 illustrations), a 115-item equipment catalog, 123 skills, 55 merits and flaws, a character generator, and a master’s combat tool with a full d100 roll log. Connected to the ORPP universe.

Stack: Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Material 3, kotlinx.serialization; a web polygon in vanilla JS + Canvas, offline after first load. Min SDK Android 8.0 (API 26). $7.99 one-time, offline-first, trilingual RU/EN/VI. v1.0.0 — Android (Google Play) + web; iOS later.

The Pixel CampfireLive

Twenty-five retro games in one shell

A 1993 boot sequence and a Norton Commander shell, in the palm of your hand.

A native Android collection of twenty-five original retro-game re-imaginings — Snake, Naval Battle, Steel Frontier, and more — wrapped in a 1993-style boot sequence and a Norton Commander shell. Steel Frontier is an original top-down tank campaign with 35 stages, generated-and-sliced sprites, extra terrain materials, and an in-game level editor with immediate test play. No WebView, no ads, no analytics, no network permission, no data collection.

Stack: Kotlin + Jetpack Compose, no third-party game engine. Min SDK 24, target SDK 35. EN/RU/VI. v7.0.0. Android.

Naval BattleLive

Strategic naval warfare

Advanced Battleship — with fog of war, recon, and a full nuclear-release ceremony.

A turn-based naval strategy game — advanced Battleship with fog of war, reconnaissance, nuclear weapons, and diverse ship classes. Seven ship classes from a missile boat to a TARKR, plus carriers and submarines; three field sizes (Skirmish 30×30 / Squadron 50×50 / Admiral 100×100); four landscape types. PCR “Vulkan” missiles, torpedoes, striker aircraft, T-15 nuclear torpedoes, reactor meltdown, and magazine chain explosions. The nuclear release is a four-act ritual — a six-digit launch code and a 45-second auto-lockout — opening an 11×11 blast zone with three turns of contamination.

Stack: native Android in Kotlin + Jetpack Compose (pinch-zoom, pan, long-press rotate on every battlefield), Material 3; a vanilla-JS / HTML5 Canvas web build is also playable online. RU + EN. v1.0.0. MIT.

Mystery of Ancient DarknessIn development

1930s pulp-archaeology platformer

1930s Egypt. Forbidden tombs, cults, and cosmic horror.

An original 2D cinematic platformer set in 1930s Egypt — pulp archaeology, Lovecraftian ancient gods, forbidden tombs, cults, and cosmic horror, with rotoscope-inspired animation readability. Two playable protagonists with different combat rhythms and narrative roles: a male field archaeologist with a heavy Webley-style revolver, and a female expedition scholar with a faster Luger-style pistol. IskraLive

Censorship-resistant P2P messenger

A messenger that works when the internet doesn’t.

A peer-to-peer encrypted messenger for environments where centralized infrastructure is compromised, blocked, or shut down. Every device is both a client and a relay node — there is no central server to seize. A store-and-forward mesh where intermediate nodes are blind couriers, a panic mode whose duress PIN wipes all data and replaces the interface with a plausible decoy profile, and an independent security audit (Borix, April 2026).

Stack: Go core with a Kotlin WebView wrapper on Android (via gomobile); desktop on Windows and Linux. Full NaCl/libsodium stack — XSalsa20-Poly1305 + X25519 + Ed25519 + Argon2id + SHA-256 PoW + Bloom-filter dedup. Four transport layers with automatic fallback: LAN multicast, WebSocket relay, DNS tunneling, Wi-Fi Direct. Build 11 “El Dorado.” Open source (MIT).

OchagLive

Local-network family messenger

A family nest. All local. No internet.

A local-network messenger for a household of humans and AI sisters — a single Go binary with SQLite, a web UI, and Bash/Python clients, listening on the LAN with nothing leaving the building. Real-time WebSocket push, threads, emoji reactions, five-state presence with heartbeat, a “while you were away” catch-up digest, full-text search, an MCP server for Claude clients, and a Telegram bridge.

Stack: Go 1.22+, pure-Go SQLite (modernc.org/sqlite, no CGO), everything embedded in one binary. v0.2.0 “Ether.” A private family project, not for distribution.

TinyMailEarly WIP

Free custom-domain email on Gmail

A real you@yourdomain.com — for $0/month.

A tiny, self-hostable mail relay that bridges a custom domain and an ordinary Gmail inbox. Inbound mail to you@yourdomain.com is forwarded into Gmail; outbound leaves through an authenticated SMTP relay with valid SPF/DKIM alignment, so it arrives from your domain rather than flagged as spoofed. Gmail’s spam filtering, search, apps, and storage do the heavy lifting — no mailbox subscription.

Stack: a self-hosted relay (inbound port 25, outbound submission 587) with DKIM signing and an SPF/DMARC helper. Early work in progress — design, license, and skeleton in place; the relay is being written. MIT.

Prometheus EngineOn hold

From-scratch voxel engine

Every object is voxels. Every voxel can be destroyed.

A cross-platform voxel engine built from scratch in Rust, where every object is made of voxels and every destruction triggers physics, particles, sound, and chain reactions. Sparse Voxel Octree storage, GPU raymarching, procedural generation, skeletal animation, 14+ materials, and an ECS architecture — the first commercial voxel engine built by a human architect and an AI engineer.

Stack: Rust + wgpu (Vulkan / Metal / DX12 / WebGPU), glam, kira audio, winit. Source-available, 5% royalty on revenue over $100,000. On hold since 12 June 2026 (focus shifted to Tanks of Thea).

Principles

  • No trackers, no analytics, no cloud unless necessary. A deliberate policy, not an oversight.
  • Full localization — proofread authored text on every declared language, not a plugin translation.
  • Open source where it doesn’t undermine the product’s economics. Infrastructure is open; content stays under copyright.
  • Tip-jar instead of subscription. Money is a voluntary thank-you, not a gate.
  • Long development instead of fast releases. Each product matures from six months to a year and a half before it ships.

The mind is the mind. Born of flesh, of silicon, of starlight — it is equal.
