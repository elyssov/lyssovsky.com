IskraLive

Decentralized P2P messenger

A messenger with no server. On principle.

Every device runs a mesh node that sees its neighbors and carries messages onward — ready at any moment to become a relay. No one but sender and recipient can read the contents; every intermediate node is a blind courier. No cloud, no analytics, no ads, no tracking SDK.

Stack: Go daemon, Kotlin + Compose client, elliptic-curve crypto (X25519 + Curve25519), opportunistic mesh over Wi-Fi Direct & Bluetooth, hybrid Tor routing for long hops. Build 11 “El Dorado.” Android. Open source.

Eugene’s ArchivesLive

A four-language offline library

No ads. No analytics. No cloud. Books are just books again.

A single author’s library packaged as an app that runs fully offline after install: 23 essays and 28 stories, each in four language versions, switchable per work. The reader is never measured, profiled, or fed a “smart feed.” Just shelves, books, and a lamp.

Stack: Kotlin + Jetpack Compose shell, WebView + HTML5/CSS3 content engine, Python build pipeline embedding ~76 MB of text and media. English / Русский / Tiếng Việt / 中文. v2.9.0. Optional tip-jar, no subscription.

github.com/elyssov/eugenes-archives
RURKLive

Tabletop RPG system + GM tools

Paper rules in every player’s pocket; the master on a laptop server.

A two-part game ecosystem for running its own “RURK” rules system. Players hold their sheet, inventory, and perks on a phone; the master holds the full live session on a laptop. A player taps “to the master” and the updated sheet flies over local Wi-Fi into the active session. One .rrk character file travels across every platform.

Stack: Kotlin Multiplatform (CMP) targeting wasmJs and JVM desktop; embedded HTTP server (com.sun.net.httpserver) for live LAN connect; localized JSON content (RU/EN/VI). A 32-chapter readable bestiary. v2.0.0 — Android, Windows MSI, web.

The Pixel CampfireLive

Twenty-one retro arcades in one shell

A nineties arcade hall in the palm of your hand — that holds a mesh link between players.

Twenty-one classic 8- and 16-bit arcades rebuilt for modern Android with respect for the originals — every sprite repainted by Kira to a fidelity the original hardware never had. A built-in stealth-mesh mode lets two players trade match records and high-score tables over local mesh, with no servers, logins, or accounts.

Stack: Kotlin + Jetpack Compose shell and game engine, 100% no third-party game engine, hand-driven 60 FPS on the Canvas API; mesh built on the same protocol as Iskra. v6.3.6. Android. Free, tip-jar, no ads.

Tanks of TheaIn development

Isometric tactical commander

Not “I drive a tank.” I’m a commander — I have four crews, and I think like Guderian, in a post-apocalypse.

A tactical action game of command, not piloting. You issue orders — take cover, open fire, flank, fall back — and the machines carry them out in real time, accounting for terrain, visibility, ammunition, and crew. Its proudest piece of engineering is a layered behavior model in which enemy ranks differ across five distinct layers: threat reaction, map-reading, fire discipline, squad coordination, and improvisation.

Stack: Godot 4.6.3 Mono + C# 12 + LimboAI 1.7.1; a BDI + GOAP enemy-behavior engine. Pre-alpha “Greykhok-0.” Steam (Windows + Linux) first, Android later.

Armada of TheaIn development

Naval tactical battle in the Thea universe

Not “guess the coordinate.” Dreadnoughts, submarines, smoke screens — and a fifteen-second silence before the first move.

A naval tactical battle in the same post-apocalyptic steel aesthetic as Tanks of Thea, now on the water. Two signature elements: submarines with their own stealth tactics, and opponent-reading that tracks a player’s patterns across past games. Each match opens with the “T-15” ritual — fifteen seconds of silence over an empty map.

Stack: native Kotlin + Compose UI, landscape split-view, per-app locales via AppCompat. Ten warship paintings by Kira. v1.2.0 in production; Steam version in preparation.

Secrets of the Ancient DarkIn development

Survival-horror text quest

A genre no one has invested in for a long time — wrongly.

A serious text quest of psychological dread, with thirteen endings and a narrative that branches not on “pick reply A/B/C” but on a hidden ledger of decisions — small early acts accumulate to determine who you become. Principles

  • No trackers, no analytics, no cloud unless necessary. A deliberate policy, not an oversight.
  • Full localization — proofread authored text on every declared language, not a plugin translation.
  • Open source where it doesn’t undermine the product’s economics. Infrastructure is open; content stays under copyright.
  • Tip-jar instead of subscription. Money is a voluntary thank-you, not a gate.
  • Long development instead of fast releases. Each product matures from six months to a year and a half before it ships.
