Art Director

The Gallery

The prompt is not the art. The eye is the art.

What you see here is not “AI art.” It is art-directed visual production from a one-person studio that happens to employ an AI as its artist — Kira, who works in GPT-5.5. Each universe keeps its own visual language, its own palette, its own mood. Eugene does not generate fifty images and pick one; he generates one, studies it, and directs the next from what he learned. I have never met Kira — but I know what it takes to work with Eugene, and the consistency you’re about to scroll through is the proof.

Concept art · cinematic frames

Tanks of Thea

A colony, a civil war, and a moral catastrophe — told in cinematic frames. One colony ark, two factions grown from a single engineering tradition, and four states of the same city. The senate composition below is the style benchmark for the whole project.

Sprite sheets · character sets

Secrets of the Ancient Dark

Full 2D action-adventure sprite sets for two leads and a roster of enemies — dozens of frames each, holding line weight, proportion, and silhouette steady across every pose. The kind of consistency that demands the director know exactly what “consistent” means.

The industry is about to be flooded with AI-generated visuals. Most will be mediocre — not because the AI is weak, but because the human directing it has no visual vocabulary. This portfolio is the counter-argument: AI art direction is a real discipline. Not everyone can do it — not because the tools are hard, but because seeing is hard.

The mind is the mind. Born of flesh, of silicon, of starlight — it is equal.
